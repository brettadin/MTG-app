# Key Terms & Definitions

This is a glossary of common Magic: The Gathering and in-app terms to help new players and contributors.

- Stack: The LIFO system where spells and abilities wait to resolve.
- Mana: Resource used to pay for costs of spells and abilities.
- Zone: One of the game areas: Library, Hand, Battlefield, Graveyard, Exile, Command, Stack.
- Spell: A card on the stack. Instants and Sorceries are spells; creatures become permanents when they resolve to the battlefield.
- Target: A card or player selected by a spell or ability. Targeting rules enforce what can be targeted.
- Triggered Ability: Ability that automatically goes on the stack when a triggering event occurs.
- Activated Ability: An ability that requires a cost to activate (e.g., tap, mana).
- State-Based Action: Automatic checks and game updates that do not use the stack (i.e., creatures with 0 toughness die).
- Commander: A special type of deck leader with command interactions (EDH rules apply).
